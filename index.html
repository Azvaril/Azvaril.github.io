Special Thermometers
Fixed Thermometers
Normal sudoku: Every row, column and 3x3 box must have all numbers from 1 to 9 exactly once.
Fixed Thermometer: Digits along the thermometers strictly increase from the bulb to the tip by a fixed step. This step may vary for other thermometers and must be determined by the solver. However, this step must never be greater than 6.
E.g. You may have one thermo: 2,4,6 (strictly increasing by 2), and a second thermo: 3,4,5 (strictly increasing by 1)
Difficulty: 6/10

Locked Mercury
Normal sudoku: Every row, column and 3x3 box must have all numbers from 1 to 9 exactly once.
German Whisper: Neighboring digits along a green line must differ by at least 5.
Black dot: A black dot between two cells indicates that one of the digits is twice as big as the other digit. Not all possible dots are given.
Fixed Thermometers: Numbers on thermometers strictly increase from the bulb to the tip by a fixed step. This step is the same for all thermometers and must be determined by the solver.
E.g. if you figured out that the numbers on a thermometer are 4-6-8, this means that the numbers on all thermometers are constantly increasing with the step of 2.

Binary Trees
Normal sudoku: Every row, column and 3x3 box must have all numbers from 1 to 9 exactly once.
Circles and lines form binary trees. One tree is a min binary tree and the other is a max binary tree. Which is which is up to the solver to determine.
Min binary tree: each parent node is smaller than its children.
Max binary tree: each parent node is larger than its children.
Digits on the tree do not repeat.
Kropki dots: A black dot between two cells indicates that one of the digits is twice as big as the other digit.
ALL possible black dots are given.
Difficulty: 8/10
